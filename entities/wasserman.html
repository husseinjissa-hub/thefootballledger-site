01 AT A GLANCE

The brand-first agency

Wasserman is the multi-sport agency founded by Casey Wasserman in 1998 in Los Angeles. The agency anchors on multi-sport representation with a particularly strong women's-football and brand-activation footprint. The football-specific roster across men's and women's football runs into the high hundreds; the broader sport roster across all athletes is materially larger.

Wasserman's commercial differentiation is brand-first athlete representation — structured commercial-revenue packages that capture longer-term commercial earnings rather than transfer-fee commission alone. The model is well-suited to women's football where transfer fees are smaller relative to commercial activation potential.

03 OPERATING REALITY

What the institution actually does

Wasserman's operating model emphasises commercial activation alongside transfer representation. The agency's senior partners typically structure athlete representations to capture brand, sponsorship, content, and adjacent commercial revenue across multi-year periods, in addition to the transfer-fee commission that dominates legacy agency economics.

The women's-football specialisation is the platform's structural differentiator. Wasserman represents the largest share of senior NWSL and WSL talent of any global agency, and the brand-activation revenue per athlete in women's football has expanded materially as franchise multiples and sponsorship inventory have grown.

The Carlyle minority investment in 2023 provided institutional capital for further roster expansion and platform-build investment. The broader Wasserman Group expanded into selected acquisitions in adjacent talent-and-sport categories alongside the core agency operating model.

04 STRATEGIC POSTURE

Direction in 2026

Wasserman's stated direction in 2026 is women's-football scaling and brand-activation deepening. The NWSL franchise multiples crossing $100m and the broader women's football institutional-capital wave provides the addressable-market expansion. The men's-football roster continues to grow at slower pace.

The Carlyle minority structure provides patient capital for the next platform-build cycle. No public IPO timeline; no signalled secondary process at the agency level.
