At a glance

A league built around one prize

The Championship is English football's second tier, the senior division of the 72-club English Football League. It is the wealthiest non-top-flight league in the world and, at roughly 22,000 average attendance, the best-attended second division outside Germany's 2. Bundesliga — a genuine standalone product whose economics are nonetheless bent entirely around promotion to the Premier League.

Two forces define it: the roughly £200m-plus value of going up, which drives chronic overspending against strict loss limits, and parachute payments to relegated clubs, which the EFL frames as a competitive distortion. Both sit at the centre of the long-running revenue-distribution fight that the new Independent Football Regulator's backstop is meant to resolve.

Operating reality

How the league actually functions

The Championship is structurally a gambling league. Clubs overspend chasing a ~£200m promotion prize, then run into a £39m three-year loss cap — a built-in tension that produces recurring points-deduction risk and a two-tier division between parachute-funded clubs and the rest. That parachute distortion is the political core of the regulatory fight, since clubs carrying relegation payments can absorb losses their rivals cannot.

World-leading attendance gives the division real product value, yet its broadcast pool — roughly £150m shared across 24 clubs — is dwarfed by a single Premier League club's central payout. That gap is the EFL's strongest factual lever in arguing for a larger settlement, and the reason the distribution dispute has escalated to a statutory regulator.

Strategic posture

Direction in 2026

The strategy is to use the Independent Football Regulator's backstop to force a larger Premier League settlement and curb the parachute distortion. As of mid-2026 no voluntary deal has been struck and the backstop is untriggered; a State of the Game review examining parachutes and pyramid distribution is due, with a final report expected in 2027. Whether the threat produces a deal or the regulator has to impose one is the defining open question.
