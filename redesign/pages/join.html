We're looking for people who understand that football is a business. Curious minds. Clear thinkers. Structured writers.

Who we're looking for

The Ledger is built by volunteers who want a foothold in the business of football. You don't need a media background — just curiosity, rigour, and a point of view. Contributors come from a wide range of places:

Business & financial analysts

Consulting, finance, or other professional backgrounds — people who can turn a deal, a filing, or a capital move into a sharp, sourced argument.

University students

Students in business, economics, law, or sports management — a chance to learn from consultants and finance professionals, and develop their knowledge of the game's economics.

Industry insiders

People working inside football, sport, media, or investment who want to write about the system from the inside.

Where you'd help

Ledger support

Researching and building articles across the nine layers of the ecosystem — from governance and ownership to media rights and matchday.

Regional coverage

Eyes on specific markets — MENA, the USA, LatAm, Asia — where the next cycle of capital is moving.

Social media

Turning published analysis into a clear, credible presence on X, LinkedIn, and Instagram.

Briefing contributor

Helping shape The Briefing — the weekly companion to our long-form analysis.

Data & research

Supporting data gathering, modelling, and visual research that underpins our pieces.

How we work

The Ledger is analysis, not reporting: neutral, third-person, evidence-led. Every piece is a structural argument with named entities, specific numbers, and a forward-looking implication. If that is how you already think about the game, you will fit here.

We value rigour over noise, structure over opinion, and curiosity over access.
How to apply

Send a note and a sample of your writing, and tell us which lane fits you best. One-off pitches are welcome — if you have a structural argument and the evidence for it, send it.
