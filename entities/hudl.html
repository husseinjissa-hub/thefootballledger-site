At a glance

The sports-performance platform

Hudl is the dominant sports-performance video and analytics platform globally, with deep penetration across US high-school and college American football alongside material global football positioning via Wyscout (acquired 2019) and the broader Hudl video-and-analytics platform. The Statsports GPS-tracking integration in 2024 extended the platform footprint into elite-football wearable-data capture.

The combined Hudl-Wyscout-Statsports architecture is the deepest sports-performance technology platform globally, serving 200,000+ teams across multiple codes and 50+ countries.

Operating reality

What the institution actually does

Hudl's operating model is subscription platform with hardware integration. The core Hudl video and analytics platform serves the broad team-sports market. Wyscout (acquired 2019) is the elite-football scouting subsidiary. Statsports (acquired 2024) is the GPS-tracking hardware-plus-platform subsidiary. Cross-platform integration is the strategic priority — combining video, scouting data, and tracking into a unified team-and-player intelligence platform.

The Bain Capital majority position since 2020 has provided capital for acquisitive integration. The 2023 financing round at $4.6 billion valuation extended capital runway for continued category consolidation.

Strategic posture

Direction in 2026

Hudl's stated direction in 2026 is integrated-platform expansion across video, scouting, and tracking. The Wyscout deepening and Statsports integration architectures are the central operating threads. Selective acquisitive posture continues across adjacent sports-performance categories.
